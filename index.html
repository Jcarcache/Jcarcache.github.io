About me

I am a Cuban American, born and raised in Florida, with a strong appreciation for both tropical and Arctic ecosystems. Outside of research, I enjoy hiking, photography, iNaturalist, and fishing. I spend much of my time thinking about the complexity of ecological interactions and how these relationships shape natural systems.

About my research

I am a first year PhD student at Florida Atlantic University who is broadly interested in symbiosis theory, plant & pollination ecology, and parasitic plants. My research focuses on symbiosis theory and how multipartite interactions can reshape how we interpret costs and benefits in parasitic plants systems along a mutulaist-parasite continuum.
